Description

Julius März is a PhD-candidate at the department of Child and Adolescence Psychiatry at Erasmus MC Rotterdam. He obtained his Psychology Research Master’s degree at the University of Amsterdam, with a specialization in Clinical and Social Psychology. The focus of his current research is the usage of Ecological Momentary Assessment data to evaluate and improve interventions that target the negative effects of daily life stress on mental health. Further research interests include the rise of science skepticism, as well as early detection of mood disorders. He is also a member of the RIOT Open Science Club Rotterdam, and a former member of the Student Initiative for Open Science (SIOS) Amsterdam.
